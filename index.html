The Sea

How it appears, your small
hand on my shoulder
under my shirt
 as I drive as if
      all at once
      I see it -
the ocean,
there, right there.

Recollection

Maybe, this morning
you are raking leaves,
scraping canals through a yard's
parched lake, that each morning,
after sleep, weigh lightly
against the ankles,
parts, as you walk, rejoin.

Fall is new here, entangles
gravity, and leaves
dervish, decomposing
into birds.

But you loved trees, didn't you? And me,
mound of rot, smoke-bleached?

What does it feel like, now, leaf-collector,
to breathe
in the magnificently ruined
colours, for a moment, decipher
the air, step through

You, wade in again, another road
peeling through,
recollecting, then, the yellow truth,
the green truth, that even leaves
had souls once, too?

Autumn Deer Again

Autumn deer, you wake me,
nonexistent mostly, or merely unbelieved,
outside the yard's chain-link fence.
Where again the others return
for the other brother who lies
quieted by leafy mud. I wonder,
does this ground open you
to be persuaded that they possess
that air which travelled so long
through them, fell, were shaken
by the old rains? Do they sign the body
as a border, beyond which lies
the outskirts of our invisible minds,
still-born creeks amid the no-man's lands?
